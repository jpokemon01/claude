# Dual US-Brazil Citizen: Owning a Brazilian Trading Company

*Comparison vs. single US citizen and Japanese national.*

---

## Short Answer

- **Brazil side: Much easier** than a single US or Japanese citizen
- **US side: Identical burden** — dual citizenship does not reduce US tax obligations

---

## What Gets Easier (Brazil Side)

| Item | Single US/Japanese | Dual US-Brazil Citizen |
|---|---|---|
| CPF | Must apply from abroad | Already have one |
| Company registration | Needs apostilled Power of Attorney | Register yourself directly |
| Resident administrator | Must hire one | You ARE the administrator |
| Bank account opening | 4–16 weeks, FATCA friction | Standard Brazilian process |
| Simples Nacional tax regime | Not available | **Available if resident in Brazil** |
| Visa / residency | Complex | No issue |
| Overall setup | Complex, expensive | Same as any Brazilian entrepreneur |

**Banking is the biggest practical improvement.** Present as a Brazilian national (CPF, RG/CNH) — processed as a standard Brazilian business account. FATCA friction largely disappears in practice.

---

## What Stays the Same (US Side)

Dual citizenship does not change your status as a **US person** for IRS purposes.

| US Obligation | Still Required? |
|---|---|
| **Form 5471** (own 10%+ of foreign corporation) | Yes |
| **FBAR (FinCEN 114)** (Brazilian bank account >USD $10,000) | Yes |
| **Form 8938** (foreign assets >USD $50,000) | Yes |
| **Form 926** (cash transfer to foreign company) | Yes |
| US worldwide income taxation | Yes |
| US CPA with international tax experience | Yes — still needed (~USD $2,000–5,000/year) |

---

## FATCA Disclosure Obligation

Brazilian banks ask on account opening forms: **"Are you a US person?"**

You are **legally obligated to answer yes.** Non-disclosure and later discovery = account closure and potential IRS/Receita Federal reporting.

In practice: Major banks (Banco do Brasil, Itaú, Bradesco) proceed with the account but add FATCA flag. The process is still faster than a foreign-only national because you have full Brazilian credentials.

---

## Key Advantage: Simples Nacional (If Resident in Brazil)

| Regime | Rate | Availability |
|---|---|---|
| **Simples Nacional** | ~6–11.28% of gross revenue | Available to dual citizen resident in Brazil |
| Lucro Presumido | ~13.33% effective rate | Foreign-owned companies (no residency) |

Simples Nacional also simplifies accounting obligations — potentially reduces contador fees. Meaningful cost saving for a trading company under BRL 4.8M/year revenue.

---

## Full Comparison

| Factor | Japanese National | Single US Citizen | Dual US-Brazil Citizen |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brazil setup complexity | Medium | Medium | Low |
| Bank account opening | 4–12 weeks | 8–16 weeks | Standard |
| Resident administrator needed | Yes | Yes | No |
| Simples Nacional available | No | No | Yes (if resident) |
| US IRS filings (5471, FBAR, etc.) | No | Yes | Yes — unchanged |
| Extra US CPA cost | No | USD $2–5K/year | USD $2–5K/year |

---

## Bottom Line

Dual citizenship is a **significant advantage on the Brazil side** — faster setup, no need to hire a resident administrator, standard banking, and Simples Nacional available if resident.

The **US tax compliance burden is completely unchanged** — the IRS does not recognize dual citizenship as reducing obligations as a US person. Budget for a US international tax CPA regardless.
