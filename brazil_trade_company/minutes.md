**User:** How to establish a trading company in Brazil

**Claude:** Researched and provided comprehensive guide:
- **Legal structure:** Use Ltda (2+ owners) or SLU (sole owner) — EIRELI abolished August 2021; no minimum capital
- **Foreign national path:** Obtain CPF online from Japan (free, 1–2 weeks) → apostilled Power of Attorney → resident administrator in Brazil; no residency required to own a company
- **Registration steps:** CPF → Contrato Social → Junta Comercial → CNPJ → Inscrição Estadual → Alvará → bank account → RADAR
- **RADAR:** Expresso (USD 50K/year), Limitado (USD 150K/year), Ilimitado (no limit); exports have no limit regardless of type
- **Product licenses:** MAPA/SIPEAGRO for coffee export; ANM provenance docs for gemstones; manga 0% tax no license; stationery may need INMETRO
- **Tax regime:** Lucro Presumido (Simples Nacional not available to foreign-owned companies); exports exempt from ICMS/PIS/COFINS
- **Banking:** 4–12 weeks to open; Banco do Brasil or BTG Pactual best for trade finance
- **Costs:** One-time BRL 3,000–10,000; monthly BRL 1,300–3,600; year-one total ~BRL 50,000–90,000
- **Timeline:** 6–8 months to first trade operation

**User:** according to brazil_trading_company_setup_guide, owner is considered as Japanese. If I am US citizen, any difference?

**Claude:** Compared US citizen vs. Japanese national ownership:
- **FATCA:** Biggest practical difference — Brazilian banks are reluctant to open accounts for US citizens; account opening takes 8–16 weeks vs. 4–12 weeks; some banks refuse US clients entirely
- **US tax obligations:** Form 5471 (10%+ foreign corp ownership), FBAR (bank balance >USD $10,000), Form 8938 (foreign assets >USD $50,000), Form 926 (cash transfers) — severe penalties for non-filing; Japanese nationals have no equivalent obligations
- **Additional cost:** US CPA with international tax experience required — USD $2,000–5,000/year extra
- **What is the same:** CPF, company structure, CNPJ, RADAR, no residency requirement, Lucro Presumido, export exemptions, product licenses, 6–8 month timeline
- **Tax treaty:** Neither Japan nor US has income tax treaty with Brazil — equal footing on Brazil side
