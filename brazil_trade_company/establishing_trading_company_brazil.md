# How to Establish a Trading Company in Brazil

*Guide for a foreign national (Japanese) setting up an export/import trading company in Brazil.*

---

## Legal Structure

**Use an Ltda or SLU — not EIRELI (abolished August 2021).**

| Structure | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **SLU** (Sociedade Limitada Unipessoal) | Single-owner limited company | Sole owner |
| **Ltda** (Sociedade Limitada) | Multi-owner limited company | 2+ owners/partners |
| SA (Sociedade Anônima) | Joint-stock company | Large companies; not recommended for small trading |

No minimum capital required by law.

---

## Can a Japanese National Own a Brazilian Company?

Yes — you do not need to live in Brazil. Non-resident path:

1. **Obtain a CPF** (Brazilian individual tax ID) — apply online from Japan; free; 1–2 weeks
2. **Apostilled Power of Attorney** — appoint a Brazil-resident attorney-in-fact to act on your behalf
3. **Resident Administrator** — Junta Comercial requires at least one Brazil-resident administrator (can be the same attorney-in-fact)

### Optional: Investor Visa (VIPER)
- Minimum investment: R$500,000
- Grants permanent residency immediately
- Worthwhile if planning to relocate to Brazil

## Step-by-Step Registration

| Step | Action | Where | Time | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Obtain CPF | Online (Receita Federal) | 1–2 weeks | Free |
| 2 | Apostille Power of Attorney | Notary in Japan | 1–2 weeks | ¥10,000–30,000 |
| 3 | Check company name availability | Junta Comercial database | 1 day | Free |
| 4 | Draft Articles of Association (Contrato Social) | Brazilian lawyer/accountant | 1–2 weeks | BRL 1,500–5,000 |
| 5 | Register with Junta Comercial | State commercial registry | 1–4 weeks | BRL 200–500 |
| 6 | Obtain CNPJ (company tax ID) | Receita Federal | 1–3 days | Free |
| 7 | State registration (Inscrição Estadual) | State tax authority | 1–2 weeks | Free |
| 8 | Municipal business license (Alvará) | City hall (Prefeitura) | 2–8 weeks | BRL 200–1,000 |
| 9 | Open business bank account | Brazilian bank | 4–12 weeks | Varies |
| 10 | Register for RADAR (import/export ID) | Receita Federal / SISCOMEX | 2–8 weeks | Free |

## RADAR Registration (Import/Export Authorization)

Required before any trade operation. Apply after CNPJ and bank account are open.

| Type | Annual Import Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Expresso** | Up to USD 50,000/year | Good for starting out |
| **Limitado** | Up to USD 150,000/year | Standard small trading company |
| **Ilimitado** | No limit | Requires liquid assets >USD 150,000 |

**Exports (coffee, gemstones) have no value limit regardless of RADAR type.**

---

## Product-Specific Licenses

### Coffee Export
- **MAPA/SIPEAGRO registration** — mandatory; register as coffee exporter
- **Phytosanitary Certificate** (VIGIAGRO) — required per shipment
- **ICO Certificate of Origin** — required per shipment (via Cecafé)
- Cecafé membership — voluntary but recommended

### Gemstone Export
- No mining license needed if buying from licensed sellers
- **ANM provenance documentation** — required (anti-money laundering rules)
- Kimberley Process Certificate — only for rough diamonds; not required for aquamarine/topaz
- IBGM membership — voluntary but useful for networking

### Manga / Books Import
- 0% tax (constitutional book immunity) — no special license required

### Stationery Import
- Standard import process; some categories require INMETRO certification

## Tax Regime

| Regime | Eligibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Simples Nacional** | Brazilian residents only | Not available to foreign-owned companies |
| **Lucro Presumido** | Any company under BRL 78M/year revenue | **Best choice for small foreign-owned trading company** |
| **Lucro Real** | Large/complex companies | Not recommended for start-up |

**Export tax advantage:** Coffee and gemstone exports are **exempt from ICMS, PIS, and COFINS** — favorable tax profile on export side regardless of regime.

## Banking

Account opening takes 4–12 weeks due to KYC on foreign-owned entities.

| Bank | Best For |
|---|---|
| **Banco do Brasil** | Trade finance, export operations |
| **BTG Pactual** | Export financing (ACC — Adiantamento de Contrato de Câmbio) |
| **Itaú** | Most accessible for foreign-owned companies |
| **Bradesco** | International transfers |
| **Nubank (PJ)** | Fastest to open; limited trade finance |

---

## Mandatory Accountant (Contador)

**Mandatory by law.** Every Brazilian company must have a CRC-licensed contador.

Monthly duties: issue Notas Fiscais, file tax returns, submit SPED digital bookkeeping files.

**Monthly cost:** BRL 800–2,500 depending on transaction volume.

## Timeline to First Trade Operation

| Month | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 1 | Obtain CPF; hire Brazilian lawyer/accountant |
| 2–3 | Company registered (CNPJ, Junta Comercial, Alvará) |
| 3–6 | Bank account opened |
| 4–7 | RADAR authorization approved |
| 5–7 | MAPA/SIPEAGRO registration (coffee) |
| **6–8** | **First trade operation possible** |

**Most common delay points:** Bank account opening (KYC on foreign entities) and RADAR authorization.
