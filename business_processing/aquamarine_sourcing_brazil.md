# Where to Buy Aquamarine (Santa Maria Color) in Brazil — Sourcing Guide

---

## The Main Mining Region: Jequitinhonha Valley, Minas Gerais

Santa Maria color aquamarine comes primarily from **northeastern Minas Gerais** — specifically the Jequitinhonha Valley. The name "Santa Maria" originally referred to the **Santa Maria de Itabira** mine in this region.

Key mining municipalities:
- **Araçuaí** — one of the most productive aquamarine areas in the world
- **Coronel Murta** — premium quality stones
- **Itinga** — active mining, direct access to producers
- **Marambaia** — historical source of finest Santa Maria color

---

## Best Place to Buy: Teófilo Otoni

**Teófilo Otoni is the gem trading capital of Brazil** — ~70% of Brazil's rough gemstone production passes through here.

| Why Teófilo Otoni | Detail |
|---|---|
| Volume | Largest rough gem market in the country |
| Price | Closest to mine-gate — 30–60% cheaper than São Paulo or Rio |
| Selection | Thousands of aquamarine lots available at any time |
| Access | Direct contact with miners and first-hand buyers |

**Where to go in Teófilo Otoni:**
- **Rua Antônio Bias Fortes** — main gem trading street; rows of dealers with rough and faceted stones
- **Mercado de Pedras** — concentrated wholesale trading

Arrive Monday–Friday morning. Dealers are most active mid-week.

## How to Identify Santa Maria Color

"Santa Maria" is a **quality grade**, not just an origin label — many dealers misuse the term.

| Grade | Color Description | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **True Santa Maria** | Deep, saturated electric blue — no green or gray tint | Premium Japan export |
| **Semi-Santa Maria** | Good blue, slight green undertone | Mid-tier Japan sales |
| **Commercial aquamarine** | Pale blue or blue-green | Avoid for export |

**Practical test:** View under natural daylight (vivid blue) and fluorescent light (must stay blue, not shift green). Avoid stones that look more green than blue in any light.

## Buying Tips

- Bring a **10x loupe** and strong flashlight — evaluate under multiple light sources
- **Buy in lots** of 10+ stones for 20–40% discount leverage
- Ask for **parcels of rough (lotes)** — much cheaper per carat than faceted
- Always get **nota fiscal** for every purchase — required for legal export
- Hire a **local broker/guide** for your first Teófilo Otoni visit — they know which dealers have genuine Santa Maria vs. commercial stock
