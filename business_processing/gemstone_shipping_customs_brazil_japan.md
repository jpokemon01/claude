# Gemstone Shipping & Customs: Brazil to Japan

---

## Shipping Options

| Method | Cost | Transit Time | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| DHL / FedEx Express | USD $50–200 per shipment (under 2kg) | 3–5 days | Small lots, high-value stones |
| Japan Post EMS | USD $30–80 per shipment | 5–10 days | Small parcels, lower value |
| Malca-Amit / Brinks | USD $200–500+ per shipment | 5–7 days | High-value lots (Paraíba, Alexandrite) |
| Air freight (cargo) | USD $8–15/kg + fees | 3–5 days | Larger lots (1kg+) |

**Standard recommendation:** DHL Express with declared value and insurance. Gems are extremely light — most parcels are under 500g.

For very high-value lots (Paraíba Tourmaline, Alexandrite), use **Malca-Amit or Brinks** — specialist gem/jewelry shippers with insurance coverage that standard couriers do not provide.

---

## Insurance

Always insure — non-negotiable for gemstones.

| Stone Value | Insurance Cost |
|---|---|
| USD $1,000–10,000 | ~1–2% of declared value |
| USD $10,000–50,000 | ~1.5–2.5% of declared value |
| USD $50,000+ | ~2–3% + specialist jeweler's policy |

## Brazil Export Costs

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Nota fiscal (receipt) | Issued by seller; no extra cost |
| SISCOMEX export declaration (RE) | Free to file; customs broker fee ~BRL 300–800 |
| GIA certificate (per stone) | USD $100–500 |
| Gemological lab report | USD $50–200 |

---

## Japan Import Duties

| Item | HS Code | Import Duty |
|---|---|---|
| Rough gemstones | 7103 | **0%** |
| Faceted / worked gemstones | 7103.91–7103.99 | **0%** |
| Gemstone jewelry | 7113 | **5.3%** on CIF value |
| Gold jewelry with gems | 7113 | **5.3%** |

**Loose gemstones (rough or faceted) enter Japan duty-free.** Only finished jewelry is taxed.

---

## Japan Consumption Tax (VAT)

- **10%** levied on all imports at Japanese customs
- Paid by Japanese buyer (B2B buyers can reclaim as input tax credit)
- Factor into pricing when selling retail direct to consumers

---

## Declaration Requirements

### Brazil — Export Side (Yes, required)

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Nota fiscal | Official receipt for every purchase — mandatory |
| SISCOMEX export registration (RE) | Required for commercial exports above USD $1,000 |
| Airport customs declaration | Required when carrying gems personally on departure |
| Personal carry limit | Goods above USD $10,000 must have export documentation; undeclared goods can be confiscated |

### Japan — Import Side (Yes, required)

| Situation | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Personal carry | Declare at Japanese customs if total goods value exceeds ¥200,000 (~USD 1,300) |
| Commercial import (shipped) | Full customs declaration required regardless of value |
| Gift exemption | Only for personal gifts under ¥200,000 — does not apply to commercial goods |

Japanese customs officers are thorough. Always declare and keep all receipts and certificates with you.

---

## Two Methods

### Option A — Ship Commercially (Recommended for Business)
1. Export from Brazil with SISCOMEX documentation
2. Ship via DHL/FedEx to Japanese buyer or importer
3. Japanese importer declares and pays applicable taxes
4. Clean paper trail for both tax authorities

### Option B — Carry Personally (Small Initial Trips / Samples)
1. Declare value at Brazilian departure customs
2. Declare at Japanese arrival customs (customs declaration form)
3. Keep all nota fiscais and GIA certificates with you
4. Practical for amounts under USD $10,000–20,000; above this use commercial shipping

## Total Cost Example

**USD $5,000 worth of faceted aquamarine, shipped DHL to Japan**

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| DHL Express shipping (~500g) | ~USD $80 |
| Insurance (1.5%) | ~USD $75 |
| Brazil export documentation | ~USD $50 |
| Japan import duty (0% loose gems) | USD $0 |
| Japan consumption tax (10%, paid by buyer) | USD $500 |
| GIA certificate | USD $150–300 |
| **Total shipping + compliance** | **~USD $200–500** |
| **As % of goods value** | **~4–10%** |

Extremely low cost relative to goods value — one of the key advantages of gemstone export over other categories.
