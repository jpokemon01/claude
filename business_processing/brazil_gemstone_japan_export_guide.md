# Brazilian Gemstones Export to Japan — Risk/Return Analysis

---

## Overview

Japan is the world's 3rd largest jewelry market (~¥1.5 trillion/year). Brazilian gemstones have strong provenance value, and Japanese consumers pay premium for rarity, certification, and origin story. This guide ranks gemstones by risk/return profile for export from Brazil to Japan.

## Tier 1 — Low Risk, High Return

### Aquamarine (Santa Maria Color)

**Best overall choice for a first-time exporter.**

- **Origin:** Minas Gerais — Brazil is world's top aquamarine producer
- **Japan price:** ¥10,000–200,000/ct
- **Why low risk:** Reliable supply, consistent quality, Brazil dominates global production
- **Why high return:** "Santa Maria" deep blue grade commands 5–10x premium over pale blue
- **Certification:** GIA recommended but not critical for mid-tier sales
- **Market:** Year-round demand (March birthstone), both jewelry and collectors
- **Competition:** Very limited — Brazil controls supply

### Imperial Topaz

**Exclusively Brazilian — zero international competition.**

- **Origin:** Only found in Ouro Preto, Minas Gerais
- **Japan price:** ¥50,000–500,000/ct
- **Why low risk:** No competing source country — provenance story is self-evident
- **Why high return:** Exclusivity drives premium; strong collector and jewelry demand
- **Certification:** GIA adds value; provenance story sells itself without certification
- **Market:** Dedicated collector base, consistent demand year-round

---

## Tier 2 — Low-Medium Risk, High Return

### Cat's Eye Chrysoberyl

- **Origin:** Minas Gerais
- **Japan price:** ¥20,000–500,000/ct
- **Why low risk:** Strong chatoyancy (eye effect) is visually obvious — easy to evaluate and sell
- **Why high return:** Japanese buyers pay superstition premium — cat's eye is considered extremely lucky
- **Certification:** Not always required for collector sales
- **Market:** Consistent demand regardless of fashion trends

### Alexandrite

- **Origin:** Minas Gerais
- **Japan price:** ¥200,000–2,000,000+/ct
- **Risk:** Sourcing stones with strong color change (80%+) is difficult — weak stones sell for a fraction
- **Why high return:** Color change (green → red) considered magical/lucky in Japan — exceptionally high demand
- **Certification:** GIA or Gübelin recommended

---

## Tier 3 — Highest Return, Highest Risk

### Paraíba Tourmaline

- **Origin:** Paraíba & Rio Grande do Norte
- **Japan price:** ¥500,000–5,000,000+/ct
- **Risk factors:**
  - Supply extremely limited and unpredictable
  - Without GRS origin certificate proving Brazilian origin, price drops 90%
  - Fakes and misrepresented stones common in market
  - Requires deep sourcing network in Paraíba state
- **Why highest return:** Rarest colored gemstone in the world by origin; copper-bearing neon blue-green unique to Brazil
- **Certification:** GRS certificate of Brazilian origin is non-negotiable

## Volume Play — Lower Per-Carat, Reliable Sales

### Amethyst

- **Origin:** Rio Grande do Sul — world's largest producer
- **Best grade:** Deep "Siberian" purple; cathedral geodes for décor market
- **Japan price:** ¥500–5,000/ct faceted; ¥30,000–300,000 for cathedral geodes
- **Risk:** Very low — unlimited supply, no certification required
- **Raw specimens:** Buy direct from miners, sell at Tokyo mineral fairs — no cutting cost

### Citrine (Madeira)

- **Origin:** Rio Grande do Sul
- **Best grade:** Madeira (deep orange-red) — 3–5x premium over pale yellow
- **Japan price:** ¥500–3,000/ct
- **Risk:** Very low

## Certifications

Japanese buyers **will not pay top price without a certificate:**

| Certificate | Best For | Price Impact | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| GRS (GemResearch Swisslab) | Paraíba — proves Brazilian origin | Non-negotiable | ¥30,000–80,000/stone |
| GIA | All colored stones | +30–100% | ¥15,000–50,000/stone |
| Gübelin | Alexandrite, Paraíba | Top-tier buyers | ¥40,000–100,000/stone |

## Recommended Starting Portfolio by Budget

### Small Budget (USD $5,000–20,000)
- **Aquamarine** (Santa Maria, 2–5ct, 20–30 stones)
- **Imperial Topaz** (1–3ct, 10–15 stones)
- Low certification cost, reliable demand, clear quality grades

### Medium Budget (USD $20,000–100,000)
- Add **Cat's Eye Chrysoberyl** (5–10 stones)
- Add **Alexandrite** (strong color change only, 5–10 stones)

### Large Budget (USD $100,000+)
- Add **Paraíba Tourmaline** with GRS certification (5–10 stones, 0.3–1ct)

## How to Enter Japan Market

| Channel | Best For |
|---|---|
| **Tokyo International Mineral Fair** (Shinjuku, June annually) | First entry — direct sales to collectors and dealers |
| **Japan Jewelry Fair** (Tokyo Big Sight, September) | Trade buyers, jewelry manufacturers |
| **Gem dealers in Ueno / Ochanomizu** (Tokyo's gem district) | Walk-in wholesale |
| **Rakuten / Yahoo! Japan auctions** | Online collector sales |

---

## Logistics

- All gems are extremely high value-to-weight → **air freight cost is negligible** relative to product value
- Use DHL or FedEx with declared value and insurance
- No special export license required from Brazil for gemstones (non-restricted minerals)
- Japan import duty on gemstones: varies by HS code (typically 0–5.3%)
