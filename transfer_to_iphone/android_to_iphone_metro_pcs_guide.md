**Scenario:** You already have an unlocked iPhone 15 purchased from eBay. You are currently on Metro by T-Mobile (Metro PCS) with an Android phone and want to keep your same phone number and plan.

## 1. Before You Start — Prepare Your Android

Do all of this **before** you begin setup on the iPhone. Once you start the transfer process, some actions on the Android will become unavailable.

### Back Up Your Contacts

- **Google Contacts (recommended):** Open the Contacts app > tap the menu (three lines) > Settings > Export or Back up. Make sure your contacts are synced to your Google account. On your iPhone, you can import them via iCloud or directly from Google.
- **Manual export:** Go to Contacts > Export > Save as .vcf file to your phone storage or Google Drive.

### Back Up Your Photos and Videos

- **Google Photos:** Install or open Google Photos on your Android. Tap your profile icon > Photos settings > Backup > turn on Backup. Wait until the backup says "Backup is on — All media backed up." This may take a while on Wi-Fi.
- Once on the iPhone, download Google Photos from the App Store and sign in with the same Google account to access all your photos.
- Alternatively, transfer photos via USB cable to a computer, then drag them onto the iPhone.

### Make a List of Your Apps

- There is no automatic app list export from Android to iOS.
- Go through your Android home screen and app drawer. Write down every app you use regularly.
- Check whether each app is available in the Apple App Store. Most major apps are on both platforms.
- Note which apps contain important data (banking, authenticator, etc.) — these need special handling (see Section 5).

### Back Up Notes

- **Google Keep:** Your notes sync to your Google account automatically. Access them on iPhone via the Google Keep app or keep.google.com.
- **Samsung Notes / other notes apps:** Export manually. Samsung Notes can export to PDF or text. Save exports to Google Drive.
- **Standard Android Notes app:** If synced to Google, accessible via Google Keep or Gmail.

### Back Up Other Important Data

- **SMS/Text messages:** The "Move to iOS" app will transfer your SMS history automatically. No separate action needed if you use Move to iOS during setup.
- **WhatsApp chats:** See Section 2 — this must be done through Move to iOS during initial iPhone setup. You cannot do it after the iPhone is already set up.
- **Google Authenticator:** Export your 2FA codes before wiping the Android (see Section 5).
- **Banking apps:** Log out of all banking apps on your Android after confirming they work on your iPhone.
- **Downloads/files:** Move any important files (PDFs, documents) to Google Drive or email them to yourself.

### Check Your Metro PCS Account Info

You will need this for the SIM/plan transfer in Section 3:

- Metro by T-Mobile phone number (your current number)
- Metro account PIN (set when you created your account, used for account changes)
- Metro account username and password (for myMetro app and website)
- Your Android phone's IMEI (dial `*#06#` on the Android to find it)

## 2. Using "Move to iOS" — The Main Transfer Tool

"Move to iOS" is Apple's free Android app that wirelessly transfers your data to the iPhone during the **initial iPhone setup**. You must use it before completing the iPhone setup — it cannot be used after the iPhone is already activated.

### What Move to iOS Transfers

- Contacts
- SMS/MMS message history
- Photos and videos (camera roll)
- Web bookmarks (Chrome bookmarks)
- Mail accounts (Gmail, etc.)
- Calendars
- WhatsApp messages and media (if selected during transfer)
- Accessibility and display settings
- Voice Memos
- Call history
- Free apps that are available on both Google Play and the App Store (they are queued for download)

### What Move to iOS Does NOT Transfer

- Music files (you must re-download from a service or transfer manually)
- Books and PDFs
- App data for most third-party apps
- Google Authenticator codes (must be exported separately — see Section 5)
- Paid apps (they must be repurchased or redownloaded from the App Store)
- App-specific login credentials

### Step-by-Step: Using Move to iOS

**On your Android phone (before starting iPhone setup):**

1. Install "Move to iOS" from the Google Play Store if you do not already have it.
2. Make sure your Android is connected to Wi-Fi and plugged into a charger.
3. Do not begin this process until the iPhone is ready (see next steps).

**On the iPhone:**

1. Power on the iPhone 15. If it was already set up, perform a factory reset: Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone > Erase All Content and Settings.
2. Follow the setup screens until you reach the "Apps & Data" screen.
3. Tap **"Move Data from Android"**.
4. A 10-digit or 6-digit code will appear on the iPhone screen.

**Back on the Android:**

5. Open the Move to iOS app and tap Continue, then agree to the terms.
6. Tap Next on the "Find Your Code" screen. Enter the code shown on the iPhone.
7. The iPhone will create a temporary private Wi-Fi network. The Android will automatically connect to it. Do not disconnect.
8. Select the content you want to transfer (Contacts, Message History, Photos & Videos, Web Bookmarks, Mail Accounts, Calendars, WhatsApp if prompted).
9. Tap **Continue** (or **Transfer** depending on your Android version).
10. Wait. The transfer can take anywhere from 10 minutes to over an hour depending on how much data you have. Keep both devices plugged in and on the same network. Do not use either phone during the transfer.
11. When the Android shows "Transfer Complete," tap Done on the Android.
12. On the iPhone, tap Continue and proceed with the remaining setup.

### WhatsApp Transfer via Move to iOS

WhatsApp chat history migration is built into Move to iOS. When you reach the data selection screen:

1. Make sure WhatsApp is up to date on your Android (update it from the Play Store first).
2. On the Move to iOS transfer screen, select **WhatsApp** from the list of items to transfer.
3. Tap **Start** on the Android — WhatsApp will prepare your data for export. You will be signed out of WhatsApp on the Android during this step.
4. Tap **Next** to return to the Move to iOS app, then tap **Continue** to proceed with the transfer.
5. After iPhone setup completes, install WhatsApp from the App Store, log in with the same phone number, and tap **Start** when prompted to restore your chat history.

**Requirements:**
- Android 5.0 or later
- iOS 15.5 or later (iPhone 15 runs iOS 17+ so this is satisfied)
- Both phones on the same Wi-Fi network during transfer

## 3. Metro PCS SIM / Plan Transfer — Activating the iPhone 15

### Important: iPhone 15 US Models Are eSIM-Only

US iPhone 15 models (iPhone 15, 15 Plus, 15 Pro, 15 Pro Max) purchased in the United States **do not have a physical SIM card tray**. They use eSIM only.

- **If your unlocked iPhone 15 was purchased in the US:** It is eSIM-only. You cannot insert a physical SIM card. You will activate it via eSIM.
- **If your iPhone 15 was purchased internationally** (e.g., European model): It has a nano-SIM tray plus eSIM support. You can use either a physical nano-SIM or eSIM.

Metro by T-Mobile fully supports eSIM on iPhone 15, and eSIM activation for existing customers is free.

### What You Need Before Activating

- Your Metro by T-Mobile account credentials (username and password)
- Your iPhone 15's IMEI number and EID (eSIM Identifier)
  - Find them by going to Settings > General > About on the iPhone (after completing initial setup), or by dialing `*#06#`
- Your current Metro phone number
- Your Metro account PIN

### Option A: Activate eSIM Online (Recommended — No Store Visit Needed)

This is the fastest method for existing Metro customers swapping to a new device.

1. **Log in to your Metro account** at [metrobyt-mobile.com](https://www.metrobyt-mobile.com) or open the **myMetro app** on another device.
2. Navigate to **My Account > Switch Device** or find the **SIM Swap** option (direct URL: `metrobyt-mobile.com/simswap.html`).
3. Select the line you want to move to the iPhone 15.
4. When prompted, enter your iPhone 15's IMEI and EID.
5. Choose **Activate eSIM**.
6. Metro will send you a QR code via email or SMS to the number on your account.
7. On your iPhone 15, go to **Settings > Cellular > Add eSIM**.
8. Tap **Use QR Code** and scan the QR code Metro sent you.
9. Follow the on-screen prompts. The eSIM will download and activate — this usually takes a few minutes.
10. When activation is complete, your Metro number and plan will be live on the iPhone 15.

### Option B: Activate via the myMetro App

1. Download and open the **myMetro** app (if you can access it on a tablet or another phone).
2. Log in to your Metro account.
3. Tap **Manage** and select the line you want to switch.
4. Choose **Switch Device** or **SIM Transfer / Activation**.
5. Enter the IMEI of the iPhone 15 when prompted.
6. Choose **Activate eSIM** and follow the prompts to receive and scan the QR code.

### Option C: Activate by Calling Metro Customer Care

- Call **1-888-8METRO8 (1-888-863-8768)**
- Tell the representative you are an existing customer and want to switch your device to an iPhone 15 using eSIM.
- Have your IMEI, EID, account PIN, and phone number ready.
- The representative will process the eSIM activation and provide you with a QR code or activation instructions.

### Option D: Activate In-Store

- Visit any Metro by T-Mobile retail store. Use the store locator at metrobyt-mobile.com.
- Bring a valid photo ID, your iPhone 15, and know your Metro account PIN.
- The store associate will activate the eSIM on your account at no charge for existing customers swapping devices.
- The in-store process typically takes 10–20 minutes.

### Do NOT Cancel Your Current Service Before Switching

Do not call Metro to cancel or suspend your current service before activating on the iPhone. The device swap process keeps your number and plan active throughout. Canceling your service early could cause you to lose your phone number.

### Confirming the eSIM is Active

After activation, on the iPhone go to **Settings > Cellular**. You should see your Metro by T-Mobile line listed as active. You can also check by making a test call or sending a text message.

## 4. iPhone 15 Initial Setup — Apple ID, iCloud, Face ID

During iPhone setup (after the Move to iOS transfer in Section 2), you will be guided through these steps. Here is what to expect and how to handle each one.

### Apple ID

- You will be asked to sign in with an Apple ID, or to create one.
- If you do not have an Apple ID, tap **Create a Free Apple ID** and follow the prompts. Use an email address you own and control.
- Your Apple ID is your identity for the App Store, iCloud, iMessage, FaceTime, and all Apple services.
- After signing in, you may be asked to verify your identity with a trusted phone number. Use a phone number you have access to.

### iCloud

- iCloud is Apple's cloud backup and sync service. It is tied to your Apple ID.
- During setup, turn on iCloud Backup. This will automatically back up your iPhone daily when it is plugged in, on Wi-Fi, and locked.
- Choose which iCloud services to enable: Photos, Contacts, Calendars, Mail, Notes, iCloud Drive. You can turn these on or off later in **Settings > [your name] > iCloud**.
- Free iCloud storage is 5 GB. If you have many photos, consider upgrading to iCloud+ (50 GB for $0.99/month, 200 GB for $2.99/month).

### Face ID

- You will be prompted to set up Face ID during setup. Do not skip it — Face ID is used to unlock your iPhone, authenticate app purchases, and enable Apple Pay.
- Follow the on-screen prompts to scan your face in two positions.
- If you wear glasses, scan with glasses on. You can also add an alternate appearance (Settings > Face ID & Passcode > Set Up an Alternate Appearance).

### Passcode

- Set a 6-digit passcode (or longer alphanumeric passcode for better security) during setup. This is required as a backup when Face ID fails or is disabled.

### Skip Options

- **Apple Pay:** You can set this up later. Skip it during initial setup if you want.
- **Siri:** Optional. Can be set up or turned off later.
- **Screen Time:** Optional. Skip unless you want parental controls.
- **True Tone, Display Zoom:** Personal preference. Can be changed later in Settings > Display & Brightness.

### After Setup Completes

- Go to **Settings > Cellular** to confirm your Metro line is active (if you already completed Section 3), or complete the eSIM activation now.
- Go to **Settings > General > Software Update** and install any available iOS updates.

## 5. What Does NOT Transfer Automatically (and How to Handle It)

These items require manual action. Handle them **before** wiping your Android or immediately after setting up your iPhone.

### Google Authenticator (2FA Codes)

Google Authenticator does not automatically sync to a new phone unless you have Google Account sync enabled in the app.

**Method 1 — Export via QR Code (works between Android and iPhone):**

1. On your Android, open Google Authenticator.
2. Tap the three-dot menu (top right) > **Transfer Accounts > Export Accounts**.
3. Select the accounts you want to transfer and tap **Next**.
4. A QR code will be displayed on the Android screen.
5. On your iPhone, install Google Authenticator from the App Store.
6. Open the app and tap **Get Started > Import Existing Accounts? > Scan QR Code**.
7. Scan the QR code shown on your Android.
8. All selected 2FA codes will be imported to the iPhone.

**Method 2 — Google Account Sync (if sync is enabled):**

1. On your iPhone, install Google Authenticator from the App Store.
2. Sign in with the same Google account you used on Android.
3. All 2FA accounts that were synced to Google will appear automatically.

**Important:** Do not factory reset or wipe the Android until you have confirmed all your 2FA codes are working on the iPhone.

### Microsoft Authenticator

Microsoft Authenticator supports cloud backup.

1. On your Android, open Microsoft Authenticator > tap the three dots > **Settings > Cloud Backup** — make sure backup is turned on.
2. On your iPhone, install Microsoft Authenticator from the App Store.
3. Sign in with your Microsoft account and choose to restore from backup when prompted.

### Banking Apps

Banking apps do not transfer data. Each app must be set up fresh on the iPhone.

1. Download each banking app from the App Store.
2. Log in using your existing online banking credentials (username and password).
3. Some banks will send a verification code to your phone number (which will now be on the iPhone — make sure your Metro number is active first).
4. Some banks may require you to visit a branch or call customer service if 2FA is tied to your old device.
5. After confirming access on the iPhone, log out and uninstall from the Android.

### Venmo / PayPal / Cash App / Zelle

These are account-based services. Simply download the app on the iPhone and log in with your existing credentials. No data loss.

### WhatsApp

Already covered in Section 2. WhatsApp can only be transferred via Move to iOS during the iPhone's initial setup. If you skipped this, you can:

- Transfer using a third-party tool such as Wondershare MobileTrans (paid).
- Or accept starting fresh on iPhone (your message history on the old Android will remain until you factory reset it).

### Spotify / Netflix / Streaming Services

These are account-based. Download the apps from the App Store and log in. All your playlists, saved content, and subscriptions carry over automatically.

### Music (Downloaded MP3s)

- If you use Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, or similar streaming services: no action needed — log in on the iPhone.
- If you have local MP3 files: connect your iPhone to a computer, open iTunes (Windows) or Finder (Mac), and drag music files in.

### Google Photos / Drive / Gmail

These work cross-platform. Download each Google app from the App Store and sign in with your Google account. All your data will be accessible.

### Contacts from Google

If your contacts are in Google Contacts (most Android users):

1. On the iPhone, go to **Settings > Mail > Accounts > Add Account > Google**.
2. Sign in with your Google account and enable **Contacts**.
3. Your Google contacts will appear in the iPhone's Contacts app within a few minutes.
4. Alternatively, during Move to iOS, contacts should have already transferred.

### Passwords / Saved Passwords

- Passwords saved in Chrome on Android can be accessed via the Chrome app on iPhone, or in your Google account at passwords.google.com.
- Consider switching to iCloud Keychain on iPhone — Safari will prompt you to save passwords as you log in to each site.

## 6. After Switching — Confirming Calls, Texts, and Remaining Data

### Confirm the iPhone is Working on Metro

1. **Make a test call:** Call a friend or family member. Confirm you can hear and be heard clearly.
2. **Send a test SMS:** Send a text message to a non-iPhone user. Confirm they receive it and can reply.
3. **Test iMessage:** Send a message to another iPhone user. iMessage should automatically activate (blue bubbles). This may take a few minutes after eSIM activation.
4. **Test mobile data:** Turn off Wi-Fi (Settings > Wi-Fi > toggle off) and open a web page in Safari. If it loads, your mobile data is working.
5. **Confirm your number:** Dial `*#06#` or go to Settings > Cellular > SIM Card (or eSIM) to confirm the correct Metro number is showing.

### Activate iMessage and FaceTime

1. Go to **Settings > Messages** and make sure iMessage is toggled on.
2. Go to **Settings > FaceTime** and make sure FaceTime is toggled on.
3. Both services will activate using your phone number and Apple ID — this usually completes within a few minutes but can take up to 24 hours.

### Notify Your Contacts

- If you were using Android's standard SMS before, your contacts may not know you now have iMessage. iMessage will be recognized automatically.
- If you previously had an iPhone and are now returning to iPhone, make sure to **deregister iMessage** if you had done so at some point (visit appleid.apple.com to check).

### Transfer Remaining Files

- If you still have files, photos, or documents on your Android, connect both phones to Wi-Fi and use Google Drive, Dropbox, or email to move them to the iPhone.
- For bulk photo/video transfers, use a USB cable and a computer as an intermediary.

### Secure and Retire Your Android

Once you have confirmed everything is working on the iPhone:

1. Make one final backup of your Android to Google Drive (Settings > Google > Backup > Back up now).
2. Factory reset the Android to protect your personal data before selling, trading in, or recycling it.
   - Samsung: Settings > General Management > Reset > Factory Data Reset.
   - Other Android: Settings > System > Reset Options > Erase All Data.
3. Remove your Google account from the Android before resetting if prompted (required by Factory Reset Protection on some devices).

### Metro Account: Update Your Device Info (Optional)

- Log into your Metro account at metrobyt-mobile.com and confirm the iPhone 15 is showing as the active device on your line.
- If you have Metro's device protection insurance, update your device information to reflect the iPhone 15.
